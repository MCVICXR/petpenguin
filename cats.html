The Cats We Have

To a pet a pet cat is strange task
A task unlike any other
Cause cats will claw your face at times
And make you cry for your mother

I found my pet cat quite on accident
I simply walked into a room
She moewed at me a sweet meow
Like Sirens leading men to doom

I simply called my cat 'the cat'
Although I gave her other names
I gave her little diminutive ones
And we'd play our silly games

The cat would nap and I would call
But she would rarely come
I would sit at my desk in the darkness
While she sat in the sun

But when I heard her suffering cries
I did whatever to ease her ills
I scrambled to fill her bowl with food
While the cat observed from windowsills

She shed her dark hair everywhere
And rubbed her scent on every surface
She'd jump from place to place to place
Like a performer in a circus

The cat came and went, meowed and fussed
And then simply left one day
I have to say, I loved that cat
I hope I see it again someday
